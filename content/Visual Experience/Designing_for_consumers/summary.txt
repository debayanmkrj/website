In this project, I worked to improve the visual shopping experience for eyewear after multiple input signals indicated that the existing image experience was not solving customer needs. I grounded the problem in 2018 research inputs and anecdotes drawn from business trends, customer reviews, user tests, and consumer behavior. From this, I defined the core problem statements: improve the eyewear shopping visual experience, address business issues caused by the image experience, and make the experience more relatable by clearly communicating product size, lens, and frame USPs.

To shape solutions, I ran a competitor mapping across marketplaces (examples shown include ASOS, Warby Parker, Lenskart, and Mrporter) and reviewed how they used multiple image variants, lifestyle/on-figure imagery, and approaches like stop-motion/360-style views and virtual try-ons. I then facilitated a “think tank” phase to brainstorm experience-design considerations around product attributes, persona, and high-consideration products, and I outlined key “elements of change” such as on-model/human interface representation and possible technology directions including stop motion and virtual try-ons.

I prototyped two experiments: introducing the human element through on-model imagery, and creating elevated product video. Although I identified Virtual Try-On (VTO) as a strong customer experience, I removed it from scope because its render accuracy, quality, scalability, and underlying technology were still evolving. For adoption, I gathered feedback through surveys with internal stakeholders and external consumers, seeing positive responses and high net-promoter outcomes. For implementation, I applied the 4M methodology (Man, Method, Machine, Material) to standardize production with detailed operating modules, quality checks, set and lighting specifications, styling guides, and SOPs for broader rollout. By 2021, I shifted the work toward scaling product videos with structured sequencing/storyboards and versions with and without voice-over, supported by qualitative and quantitative analysis.
