In this project, I built Warrior Duel, a side-scrolling 2D fighting game where I play as one of three warrior characters—Fighter, Samurai, or Shinobi—and battle vampire enemies across three increasingly difficult levels. I designed the experience around a clear loop: I start at the home screen, select a character, enter the battle, and progress through a sequence of scenes from Main Menu → Character Select → Level 1 → Level 2 → Level 3. During combat, I implemented a health system and a time-based match structure (a 100-second limit per battle), along with a special meter that builds as I land attacks and unlocks a powerful special move.

I created an on-screen virtual controller so the game is playable with touch controls: a left joystick for movement (with running triggered after holding movement for a second), two attacks (basic punch and stronger kick), a special button, and a shield button that reduces incoming damage by 70%. I also built the game using a scene-based architecture with dedicated modules for the menu, character selection, HUD, and per-level logic. For gameplay feel, I implemented combat using a physics-based collision system with custom hit detection for attacks and defensive maneuvers. Finally, I added adaptive enemy behavior where enemies can chase, block, attack when in range, and trigger special attacks when their meter is full, scaling difficulty across the three levels.

Tech Stack (from GitHub)
