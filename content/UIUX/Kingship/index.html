Accessible FinTech · Voice-first banking

A banking app that speaks plainly, listens carefully, and never hides the exit.

Kinship is a multimodal banking concept for older adults and the unbanked — voice-first by default, touch-first when needed, and built around massive hit targets, high contrast, and safe clarification before money moves.

Micro-interactions + motion logic

Motion is subtle and functional: the voice orb pulses while listening, confirmation cards brighten when details are complete, and motor-assist mode grows target sizes rather than adding visual chrome.

Accessibility + VUI flow

Accessibility audit built into the concept

The UI layers in large touch targets, high-contrast surfaces, big type, and obvious support lanes from the start — not as a QA pass.

Accessibility audit
  • Low vision: large numerals, high contrast, and minimal decorative noise.
  • Motor support: oversized hit targets, forgiving spacing, imprecision-tolerant chip groups.
  • Cognitive load: plain language, one primary action per screen, no gamified streaks or confetti.
  • Human fallback: support is visible in the same lane as confirmation and receipts.

Voice-first banking webflow

The interaction flow treats spoken intent as the starting point, not the whole experience. Ambiguity triggers clarification, then the touch UI takes over with big, accessible confirmations.
Webflow / interaction map
Hear request

The user speaks or taps a saved utterance like "Move some money to my sister."

Intent parse

Kinship identifies transfer, payment, or insight without guessing missing financial details.

Clarify safely

The UI asks one question at a time to resolve recipient, amount, or date ambiguity.

Plain confirm

Before money moves, the action is read back in direct everyday language.

Execute

The payment or transfer runs only after explicit confirmation.

Receipt + help
