Micro-interactions + motion logic

Motion is spatial: anchors ripple in place, cards float between virtual distances, and the wrist bag bumps when an item gets pulled out of the scene.

Anchor ripple Each discovered item pings softly so the user notices without seeing a pop-up.
Depth drift Cards shift scale and shadow as they move between depth layers.
Pinch + pull A save action creates a quick arc into the wrist bag instead of a flat toast.
Scan beam A passing scan line reinforces that the scene is being mapped in real space.

Key portfolio proof

For spatial commerce the portfolio has to prove two things: the interface respects depth, and the gesture vocabulary feels learnable enough to use while walking.

Custom hand gestures with physical logic

Gestures are designed to feel like moving objects through space rather than tapping a floating phone UI in the air.

Gesture vocabulary
  • Pinch: preview the compact card without committing to anything.
  • Pinch + pull: save the item to the wrist bag with a directional motion.
  • Palm flip: open the wrist bag close to the body.
  • Gaze dwell: expand from glance info into richer product detail.
